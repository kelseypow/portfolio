Recycling For Kids (2019)


Senior Capstone Project

•Goal was to design safe, economical tools with which children may more easily prepare cardboard for recycling
•Before puberty, many children lack the strength required to fold or tear cardboard to make it fit in a recycling bin; additionally, many parents don't want to give sharp tools to their young kids
•The Perforating Wheel, Cardboard Punch, and Cardboard Saw were designed to ergonomically fit in the hands of children aged 5-10, and to conform with relevant ASTM and US CPSC standards
•SolidWorks design allowed for rapid prototyping via 3D printing and multiple iterations of tools
•Personal responsibilities included complete CAD design of Perforating Wheel and Cardboard Punch tools at all iterations, and safety testing
•Tools were tested with members of the target market at Cub Scout meetings and Boys & Girls Club events to gain feedback for design improvement
•CAD files may be freely downloaded from GrabCad and 3D printed directly by consumers to cut down on carbon footprints associated with manufacturing, packaging, and shipping

Winched Loft Bed (2019)


Final Project for Mechanical Engineering Design course

•Goal was to design a novel mechanical system that fills a market need
•This product is intended for students or young professionals living in small urban apartments who want to utilize the most space in their bedrooms
•A freestanding frame and winch system allows the bed to travel vertically on cables, guided by a rack and pinion system, to get out of the way when not in use
•Space underneath could be used for a desk, extra seating, crafts, etc.
•Personal responsibilities included static/dynamic loading and failure analysis of the pulley subassembly and all attachments such as aircraft cable, eye bolts, and rivets

Hydrophone Mounts (2017)


Co-op project at General Dynamics Electric Boat

•Goal was to calculate stress, strain, and deformation on Hydrophone mounting brackets for the upcoming Columbia class of US Naval submarines
•Analysis was performed both by hand, using MathCAD, and by FEA software in HyperMesh and Abaqus
•Machining specifications were consulted to ensure that brackets were being created in accordance with Design For Manufacturing (DFM) principles
•Analysis results were discussed with the design team for multiple iterations, and the final design package was presented to senior management in the department
•This project applied static loading skills, adding to technical literacy in both mathematics and FEA software
•These brackets, designed in the Spring of 2017, will be manufactured sometime after 2021, with completion of the first Columbia class vessel scheduled for 2030
•Due to the classified nature of this project, more specific information may only be discussed in person and work samples may not be shared
